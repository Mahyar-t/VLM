About Me

I'm Mahyar Taherpour (PhD), a scientific software and machine-learning engineer. My work sits where physics, simulation, and production-quality software meet. I build tools that help researchers and engineers turn ideas into models, and models into results they can trust.

I earned my PhD in Applied Computational Physics at Eindhoven University of Technology, where my research focused on accurate and fast modeling of opto-electronic processes and advanced simulation methods. Before that, I completed an M.Sc. in Solid-State Physics at the University of Tehran, working on charge transport modeling in solar-cell materials.

Over the past years, I've worked on high-performance simulation software and ML-driven engineering systems, using tools like Python and C++, numerical solvers, and scalable computing. I also apply practical software engineering practices — including testing, clean architecture, and CI/CD — so the final product is not just a demo, but something people can actually use.

This website is built around that same goal: a web-based simulation tool that makes it easier to explore designs, test assumptions, and learn faster without needing heavy setup or long trial-and-error cycles for scientific users.

If you'd like to collaborate, give feedback, or discuss use-cases, feel free to reach out via my LinkedIn, GitHub, or contact page.
